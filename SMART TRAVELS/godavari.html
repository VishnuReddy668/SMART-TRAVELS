About Godavari

The Godavari River, also known as the "Dakshina Ganga" or "Southern Ganges," is one of the major rivers of India. It flows through five states: Maharashtra, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha. The Godavari is considered sacred by Hindus and plays a significant role in Indian mythology and culture.

Origin and Course

The Godavari River originates in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra and flows eastward through the Deccan Plateau. It empties into the Bay of Bengal, forming a large delta. The river's course is marked by numerous tributaries and stunning landscapes.

Religious Significance

The Godavari River holds immense religious significance for Hindus. It is considered a sacred river and is associated with several Hindu deities, including Lord Shiva, Lord Vishnu, and Goddess Parvati. Many Hindu festivals and rituals are celebrated along the banks of the Godavari.
