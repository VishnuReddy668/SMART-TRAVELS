About Burra Caves

Burra Caves, located in the Kurnool district of Andhra Pradesh, are a group of natural caves with significant historical and archaeological importance. These caves are known for their prehistoric rock paintings and inscriptions, offering a glimpse into the lives of ancient humans.

History

The Burra Caves are believed to date back to the Mesolithic and Neolithic periods. The rock paintings and inscriptions found within the caves depict hunting scenes, human figures, and geometric patterns. These artifacts provide valuable insights into the lifestyle and culture of the early inhabitants of the region.

How to Reach

Burra Caves is well-connected by road. The nearest major railway station is Kurnool.
