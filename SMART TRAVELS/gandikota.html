About Gandikota

Gandikota, often referred to as the "Grand Canyon of India," is a picturesque village nestled in the Anantapur district of Andhra Pradesh. Known for its stunning gorge carved by the Penna River, Gandikota offers breathtaking views and a serene atmosphere.

History

Gandikota has a rich historical past, dating back to the 13th century. It was once a fortified village under the Vijayanagara Empire. The remnants of the Gandikota Fort, including the Virupaksha Temple and the Rayalaseema University, stand as testaments to its historical significance.

Attractions

  • Gandikota Gorge
  • Virupaksha Temple
  • Rayalaseema University
  • Penna River
  • Hiking and Trekking Trails

How to Reach

Gandikota is well-connected by road. The nearest major railway station is Anantapur, and the nearest airport is Bengaluru.
